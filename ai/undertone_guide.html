🎨 Skin Undertone Labeling Guide

❄️ COOL Undertone

Characteristics:

  • Skin has pink, red, or bluish hues
  • Veins appear blue or purple on wrist
  • Silver jewelry looks better than gold
  • Skin burns easily in sun, rarely tans

Color References:

☀️ WARM Undertone

Characteristics:

  • Skin has yellow, peachy, or golden hues
  • Veins appear greenish on wrist
  • Gold jewelry looks better than silver
  • Skin tans easily, rarely burns

Color References:

⚖️ NEUTRAL Undertone

Characteristics:

  • Mix of pink and yellow, balanced
  • Veins appear both blue and green or unclear
  • Both silver and gold jewelry look good
  • Skin tans and burns moderately

Color References:

🫒 OLIVE Undertone

Characteristics:

  • Greenish or ashy tint to skin
  • Veins appear blue-green (teal)
  • Both metals work, but rose gold is best
  • Common in Mediterranean, Latin, Asian skin

Color References:

📝 How to Label:

  1. Open each image and look at the skin color
  2. Check if it's more pink (Cool), yellow (Warm), balanced (Neutral), or greenish (Olive)
  3. Also determine skin depth: Light, Medium, or Deep
  4. Place the image in the corresponding folder: light_cool/, medium_warm/, etc.
